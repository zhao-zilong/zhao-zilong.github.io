About Me

I'm a Postdoc in EECS at TU Delft since Jul. 2020, where I collaborate with Prof. Lydia Y. Chen. I did my PhD at GIPSA-lab of University of Grenbole Alpes between 2017 and 2020. I worked on integrating control theory into machine learning (ML) algorithm. Meanwhile, during the same period, I remotely served as a data engineer lead and data scientist for Miro Health (a San Francisco seed-round digital health start-up). I helped to optimize a speech recognition system into IOS system, use ML algorithms to analyze mild cognitive impairment (MCI) patients' data. I also represented Miro Health to research with John Hopkins University, University of Pennsylvania, and UCLA.

My current research is focused on Generative Adversarial Networks (GANs) for tabular data and training GANs in privacy-preserving environments. I am currently in charge of a pioneer research collaboration between our group and Tampere University. Our goal is to use GANs to learn and generate molecules under specific conditions.
